Some parents who are claiming asylum are eligible for free school meals under separate eligibility criteria. These families may not have a National insurance number and cannot be processed through our standard online free school meals checking system.

To ensure fair access, local authorities must use the offline form to check eligibility for these families.

Accessing the offline form

Local authorities must use the approved offline free school meals application form for asylum seekers.

This document is classified as OFFICIAL-SENSITIVE. Only authorised local authority staff should access and complete it.

Access the official free school meals application form (authorised users only)

When to use the offline form when the parent or guardian:

  • is seeking asylum
  • does not have a National insurance number
  • cannot be verified through the online system
  • meets the free school meals criteria under asylum support arrangements

How local authorities should support families

You must:

  • provide information about the offline form and how to complete it
  • offer assistance to families who may have difficulty completing the form
  • request supporting evidence confirming asylum support status
  • ensure that the form is submitted to the Home Office for confirmation
  • communicate the outcome of the application to the family in a timely manner

Submitting and storing the form

Local authorities must ensure that the offline form is submitted securely and stored appropriately. This includes:

  • using secure methods to submit the form to the Home Office
  • keeping a copy of the submitted form and any supporting evidence
  • storing all documents securely to protect the family's privacy

Help and support

For further help and support, contact our support team using the Customer help portal.
