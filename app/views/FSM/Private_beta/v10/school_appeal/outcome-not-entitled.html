The children of this parent or guardian may not be eligible for free school meals.

From the information you’ve provided we cannot confirm if the children of this parent or guardian are eligible for free school meals.

If the parent or guardian thinks their children are eligible

You can make an appeal on their behalf, but you'll need to:

  • ask the parent or guardian to send you their supporting evidence
  • send their supporting evidence to your local authority to review

You can upload the evidence when you appeal or email it to your local authority afterwards.

Complete list of acceptable evidence

You can send:

  • up to three latest Universal Credit statements
  • their latest tax credit statement form TC602A (Award Notices) or TC603R (Auto Renewal), showing they are receiving Working Tax Credit run-on
  • their latest tax credit statement form TC602A (Award Notices) or TC603R (Auto Renewal), showing they are receiving Child Tax Credit
  • a letter of entitlement for Income Support
  • a letter of entitlement for income-based Jobseeker’s Allowance
  • a letter of entitlement for the guaranteed element of Pension Credit
  • documents showing they are receiving relevant support under part VI of the Immigration and Asylum Act 1999

More information on supporting evidence.

Alternatively, you can contact the Department for Education support desk at ecs.admin@education.gov.uk to request a separate check.
