To find out if the children of a parent or guardian are entitled to free school meals, you’ll need to review the supporting evidence they've provided.

Choose the relevant section from the evidence list for tailored guidance on how to review.

Universal Credit statements

If the parent or guardian provides Universal Credit statements, they are entitled to free school meals.

Important: Any statements provided must show the parent/guardian’s income and the date it was sent.

Their latest statement should refer to the month immediately before the date of their request.

They are entitled to targeted free school meals if:

  • the take-home pay in their latest Universal Credit statement is less than £616.67
  • the take-home pay for their most recent statement + the one covering the month directly before = less than £1,233.34
  • the take-home pay from their most recent statement + the one covering the month directly before + the one covering the month directly before that = less than £1,850.00

In line with these checks, the annual net earned income of the parent or guardian must be no higher than £7,400.

It’s preferable that you conduct all three checks if possible. However, if you don’t have three consecutive Universal Credit statements, you can use any combination of the checks to make a decision.

You can also read more in-depth free school meals guidance on GOV.UK.

Support under part VI of the Immigration and Asylum Act 1999

When checking an applicant with an asylum support reference number (formerly NASS number), a document proving they are receiving relevant support under part VI of the Immigration and Asylum Act 1999 is needed. This may include:

  • a letter from the Home Office
  • a visa or student visa
  • other kinds of documentation, to be reviewed at your discretion

If the document provided demonstrates that they receive such support, this is sufficient evidence for them to claim free school meals for their children.

Note that some asylum seekers with a support reference number are claiming asylum under part IV of the Immigration and Asylum Act 1999. These claimants are not eligible.

You can also read more in-depth free school meals guidance on GOV.UK.

Employment and Support Allowance (ESA)

To see if a parent or guardian can claim free school meals for their children, check their Letter of Entitlement from the Department for Work and Pensions, or Income Support payment book.

An applicant who is in receipt of income-related Employment and Support Allowance (ESA) may be entitled to free school meals.

Pension Credit

To see if a parent or guardian can claim free school meals for their children, check their Letter of Entitlement from the Department for Work and Pensions. This will typically be a Pension Credit M1000 Award Notice.

If the letter states they are currently in receipt of guarantee element of Pension Credit, they may be entitled to free school meals

You can also read more in-depth free school meals guidance on GOV.UK.
