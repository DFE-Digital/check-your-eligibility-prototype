Your children may not be entitled to free school meals

Based on the information you’ve provided, we cannot confirm if your children are entitled to free school meals.

If you haven’t received any evidence yet, please ask the parent/guardian to provide it.

If you think that they are definitely are entitled, you can show supporting evidence to your school to help us re-assess.

Accepted kinds of supporting evidence

Important: Please do not send any documents which do not meet the criteria that follows.

To support your claim for free school meals, you can send your:

  • latest Universal Credit statements (up to three).

Important: Your annual net earned income must be no higher than £7,400.

Any documents you provide must show your income and the date.

  • latest tax credit statement form TC602A (Award Notices) or TC603R (Auto Renewal), showing you are receiving Working Tax Credit run-on

  • latest tax credit statement form TC602A (Award Notices) or TC603R (Auto Renewal), showing you are receiving Child Tax Credit

Important: You must not be entitled to Working Tax Credit and your annual gross income must be no more than £16,190.

  • Letter of Entitlement for Income Support
  • Letter of Entitlement for income-based Jobseeker’s Allowance
  • Letter of Entitlement for the guaranteed element of Pension Credit

Typically a Pension Credit M1000 Award Notice.

  • document showing you are receiving relevant support under part VI of the Immigration and Asylum Act 1999

This is typically a letter from the Home Office.

Further guidance

More in-depth information on supporting evidence can be found in the guidance notes for parents and guardians.
