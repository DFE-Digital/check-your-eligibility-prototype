The children of this parent/guardian may not be entitled to free school meals

To confirm, you’ll need to send their supporting evidence to your local authority for review.

If you haven’t received any evidence yet, please ask the parent/guardian to provide it.

Accepted kinds of supporting evidence

Important: Please do not accept any documents which do not meet the criteria that follows.

To support their claim for free school meals, parents/guardians can send their:

*latest Universal Credit statements (up to three).

Important: Their annual net earned income must be no higher than £7,400.

Any documents they provide must show their income and the date.

*latest tax credit statement form TC602A (Award Notices) or TC603R (Auto Renewal), showing they are receiving Working Tax Credit run-on

*latest tax credit statement form TC602A (Award Notices) or TC603R (Auto Renewal), showing they are receiving Child Tax Credit

Important: They must not be entitled to Working Tax Credit and their annual gross income must be no more than £16,190.

*Letter of Entitlement for Income Support

*Letter of Entitlement for income-based Jobseeker’s Allowance

*Letter of Entitlement for the guaranteed element of Pension Credit

Typically a Pension Credit M1000 Award Notice.

*document showing they are receiving relevant support under part VI of the Immigration and Asylum Act 1999

This is typically a letter from the Home Office.

Further guidance

More in-depth information on supporting evidence can be found in the guidance notes for parents and guardians.
