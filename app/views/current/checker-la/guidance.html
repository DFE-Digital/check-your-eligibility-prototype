Guidance for reviewing evidence

To find out if the children of a parent/guardian are entitled to free school meals, you’ll need to review the supporting evidence they've provided.

Choose the relevant section from the evidence list for tailored guidance on how to review.

Universal credit statement(s)

To see if a parent/guardian can claim free school meals for their children, you must find their take-home pay in the Universal Credit statement(s) they’ve provided.

Important: Any statements provided must show the parent/guardian’s income and the date it was sent.

Their latest statement should refer to the month immediately before the date of their request.

They are entitled if:

  • the take-home pay in their latest Universal Credit statement is less than £616.67
  • the take-home pay for their most recent statement + the one covering the month directly before = less than £1,233.34
  • the take-home pay from their most recent statement + the one covering the month directly before + the one covering the month directly before that = less than £1,850.00

In line with these checks, the annual net earned income of the parent/guardian must be no higher than £7,400.

It’s preferable that you conduct all three checks if possible. However, if you don’t have three consecutive Universal Credit statements, you can use any combination of the checks to make a decision.

You can also read more in-depth free school meals guidance on GOV.UK.

National Asylum Support Service (NASS) evidence

When checking an applicant with a National Asylum Support Service (NASS) number, a document proving they are receiving relevant support under part VI of the Immigration and Asylum Act 1999 is needed. This may include:

  • a letter from the Home Office
  • a visa or student visa
  • other kinds of documentation, to be reviewed at your discretion

If the document provided demonstrates that they receive such support, this is sufficient evidence for them to claim free school meals for their children.

Note that some asylum seekers with a NASS number are claiming asylum under part IV of the Immigration and Asylum Act 1999. These claimants are not eligible.

You can also read more in-depth free school meals guidance on GOV.UK.

Income Support

To see if a parent/guardian can claim free school meals for their children, check their Letter of Entitlement from the Department for Work and Pensions.

If the letter states they are currently in receipt of Income Support, they also count as being entitled to free school meals.

You can also read more in-depth free school meals guidance on GOV.UK.

Income based Jobseeker's Allowance (JSA)

To see if a parent/guardian can claim free school meals for their children, check their Letter of Entitlement from the Department for Work and Pensions.

If the letter states they are currently in receipt of income based Jobseeker’s Allowance (JSA), they also count as being entitled to free school meals.

You can also read more in-depth free school meals guidance on GOV.UK.

Pension Credit

To see if a parent/guardian can claim free school meals for their children, check their Letter of Entitlement from the Department for Work and Pensions. This will typically be a Pension Credit M1000 Award Notice.

If the letter states they are currently in receipt of Pension Credit, they also count as being entitled to free school meals.

You can also read more in-depth free school meals guidance on GOV.UK.

Child Tax Credit

The parent/guardian needs to provide their latest tax credit statement form TC602A (Award Notices) or TC603R (Auto Renewal). The statement must be for the most recent tax year.

They are entitled to claim free school meals for their children if:

  • Child Tax Credit is in payment
  • Working Tax Credit is not also in payment
  • their annual gross income is no more than £16,190

Alternatively, you can contact the DfE support desk on ecs.admin@education.gov.uk to arrange a separate check.

You can also read more in-depth free school meals guidance on GOV.UK.

Working Tax Credit

The parent/guardian needs to provide their latest tax credit statement form TC602A (Award Notices) or TC603R (Auto Renewal). The statement must be for the most recent tax year.

If either of these forms show that Working Tax Credit run-on is in payment, then they are entitled to claim free school meals for their children.

Alternatively, you can contact the DfE support desk on ecs.admin@education.gov.uk to arrange a separate check.

You can also read more in-depth free school meals guidance on GOV.UK.
