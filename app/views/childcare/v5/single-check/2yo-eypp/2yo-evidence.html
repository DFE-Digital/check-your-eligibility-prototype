Early learning for 2-year-olds

Guidance for reviewing evidence

To find out if the children of a parent or guardian are entitled to early learning for 2-year-olds, you’ll need to review the supporting evidence they've provided.

Choose the relevant section from the evidence list for tailored guidance on how to review.

Universal Credit statements

To see if a parent or guardian can claim early learning for 2-year-olds, you must find their take-home pay in the Universal Credit statement(s) they’ve provided. If a ‘minimum income floor’ has been applied by the Department for Work and Pensions, you should use the actual earnings instead.

Important: Any statements provided must show the parent/guardian’s income, and the income of any partner listed on the claim, and the date it was sent.

Their latest statement should refer to the Universal Credit assessment period immediately before the date of their request.

They are entitled if any of the following apply:

  • the take-home pay of the applicant and any partner listed in the latest Universal Credit statement is £1283.33 or less
  • the take-home pay of the applicant and any partner listed in the latest Universal Credit statement + the one covering the Universal Credit assessment period directly before is £2566.67 or less
  • the take-home pay of the applicant and any partner listed in the latest Universal Credit statement + the one covering the month directly before + the one covering the month directly before that is £3850.00 or less

Families with no recourse to public funds (NRPF)

To see if a family can claim early learning for 2-year-olds, check their signed application form confirming both their immigration category and that they are not above the maximum income threshold required for their area.

Families can use the DfE sample application form, which also includes details of the income thresholds, or they can use one created by a local authority.

You can confirm proof of NRPF status from one of the following:

  • the online Immigration Checking Service
  • an Application Residence Card (ARC)

To verify income, check one of the following for the applicant and their partner (if they have one), covering the last 3 months:

  • payslips
  • P60
  • bank statements
  • letter from their bank confirming total incoming funds
  • letter from their employer confirming monthly salary

You must also confirm that the applicant and their partner (if they have one) do not have more than £16,000 in capital (this includes savings, investments or other assets).

If an applicant is an asylum seeker that receives financial support, you can ask to see a copy of a letter from the Home Office confirming that they have been awarded financial support.

You can also read more in-depth guidance on GOV.UK.

Children in care

To see if children in care are eligible for early learning for 2-year-olds, you can check with the local authority social care team to confirm that they are looked after or previously looked after by a local authority.

Children who have left care via adoption, a special guardianship order or a child arrangements order are eligible for early learning for 2-year-olds.

Education, health and care (EHC) plan

To see if a parent or guardian can claim early learning for 2-year-olds, check with the local authority to confirm that they have an EHC plan.

Disability Living Allowance

To see if a parent or guardian can claim early learning for 2-year-olds, check their Letter of Entitlement from the Department for Work and Pensions.

If the letter states they are currently in receipt of Disability Living Allowance, they also count as being entitled to early learning for 2-year-olds.

Income Support

To see if a parent or guardian can claim early learning for 2-year-olds, check their Letter of Entitlement from the Department for Work and Pensions.

If the letter states they are currently in receipt of Income Support, they also count as being entitled to early learning for 2-year-olds.

Income based Jobseeker's Allowance (JSA)

To see if a parent or guardian can claim early learning for 2-year-olds, check their Letter of Entitlement from the Department for Work and Pensions.

If the letter states they are currently in receipt of income-based Jobseeker’s Allowance (JSA) - sometimes called old style JSA - they also count as being entitled to early learning for 2-year-olds.

Income-related Employment and Support Allowance (ESA)

To see if a parent or guardian can claim early learning for 2-year-olds, check their Letter of Entitlement from the Department for Work and Pensions.

If the letter states they are currently in receipt of income-related Employment and Support Allowance (ESA) - sometimes called old style ESA - they also count as being entitled to early learning for 2-year-olds.

Pension Credit

To see if a parent or guardian can claim early learning for 2-year-olds, check their Letter of Entitlement from the Department for Work and Pensions. This will typically be a Pension Credit M1000 Award Notice.

If the letter states they are currently in receipt of Pension Credit, they also count as being entitled to early learning for 2-year-olds.
